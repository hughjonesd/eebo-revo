Colures.</xpln></wordentry>

Longitude.
</note>

<note type="lm">
The Zodiake,
</note>
<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="zodiac(n)">The Zodiake </form> <xpln lexeme="ecliptic(a)" lexeme="line(n)" lexeme="shoring(a)">is a great, broad, and slope or shoring

<note type="lm">
The Meridian
</note>
<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="meridian(n)">The Meridian </form> <xpln lexeme="noontide(n)">is a greate Circle, passing ouer our
heades, in what parte of the World soeuer we be, and al&#173;

toucheth it aboue the Horizon, it is Noonetide or midday
to those that dwell vnder the same.</xpln></wordentry>

<note type="lm">
The Horison.
</note>
<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="horizon(n)">The Horizon </form> <xpln>is a great Circle, deuiding the vpper
halfe of the World which we see, from the nether halfe
which wee see not: in <emend err="the the">the</emend> very middest or Center of
which Circle, if in a plaine field you looke rounde about
you, you shall alwaies finde your selfe to be.</xpln></wordentry>
