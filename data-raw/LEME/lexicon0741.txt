<section type="preface">
<heading>
TO THE

READER.
</heading>

<f type="bk">T<f type="i"><f type="i">His<f type="r"> Dictionary, <f type="i">above any other part of this Book, may be
thought superfluous, because it being intended onely for the use
of<f type="r"> Husbandmen, <f type="i">they above all other best understand the
Terms, and their several significations, so that herein we seem to in&shy;
struct those that are best able to teach us, which might be true if they all
spoke the same language, but there is such a<f type="r"> Babel <f type="i">of confusion as well
in their Terms and Names of things, as there is in the practice of the Art
of<f type="r"> Agriculture <f type="i">it self, that remove a<f type="r"> Husbandman <f type="i">but sixty, or an
hundred Miles from the place where he hath constantly exercised his<f type="r">
Husbandry, <f type="i">to another, and he shall not onely admire their Method and
Order in Tilling the Land, but also at their strange and uncouth Lan&shy;
guage and Terms, by which they name their several<f type="r"> Utensils, Instru&shy;
ments, <f type="i">or<f type="r"> Materials <f type="i">they use; Also our several<f type="r"> Authors <f type="i">that have writ&shy;
ten of this<f type="r"> Subject, <f type="i">very much differ in the Appellation of several things,
they generally speaking in their Writings, the<f type="r"> Language <f type="i">of the<f type="r"> Place
<f type="i">and<f type="r"> Age <f type="i">they lived in, that their Books read in another part of the
Countrey, or in succeeding Times, seem either fabulous or intricate;
wherefore that our<f type="r"> Authors, <f type="i">and this present<f type="r"> Tract, <f type="i">may be the better
understood, and also that one<f type="r"> Countrey-man <f type="i">may understand what an&shy;
other means in a remote place, I have here given you the<f type="r"> Interpretation,
<f type="i">and<f type="r"> Signification <f type="i">of such<f type="r"> Words <f type="i">and<f type="r"> Terms <f type="i">that I remember I have
either read or heard, which I hope may satisfie, and supply that defect
of such a<f type="r"> Dictionary <f type="i">that hath been so long complained of. If any Terms
are wanting, or not rightly interpreted, I desire you to consider the place
you live in, where perhaps may be some<f type="r"> Terms <f type="i">used, or so<f type="r"> interpreted,
<f type="i">that are not so in any other place of<f type="r"> England, <f type="i">which may I hope suffi&shy;
ciently excuse my ignorance of them; Or else they may be<f type="r"> Terms <f type="i">so uni&shy;
versally understood that they need no<f type="r"> interpretation, <f type="i">as<f type="r"> Wheat, Rye,
Cart, Waggon, <f type="i">&amp;c.<f type="r">
</section>
