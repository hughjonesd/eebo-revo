<section type="glossary">
<heading>AN
EXPLANATION
OF THE
MINERS
Terms of Art
Used in this
BOOK.</heading>

<wordgroup1 type="a">
<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="bar-master(n)"><f type="bk">B<f type="i">Ar-master<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is he which keepeth
the Gage or Dish to measure
all Miners Ore, he or his Servant be&shy;
ing present when measured.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="brazen-dish(n)"><f type="i">Brazen-Dish<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is the Standard by
which the other Dishes are gaged,
and is kept in the King's Hall.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="caver(n)"><f type="i">Cavers<f type="r"> </form><xpln>are any poor people that
go about the Mines to beg or steal
Ore from the Miners.</xpln></wordentry>
</page>

<page sig="f1v">
<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="coe(n)"><f type="i">Coes<f type="r"> </form><xpln>are little Houses which the
Miners make over their Mines to
lay the Ore in.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="cope(n)"><f type="i">Cope<f type="r"> </form><xpln lexeme="load(n)">is <f type="i">6d<f type="r">. for every Load of Ore,
nine Dishes making one Load.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="cross(n)"><f type="i">Crosses<f type="r"> </form><xpln>are two Nicks cut on the
Superficies of the Earth, thus &#9769;
which the <f type="i">Miners<f type="r"> make when they
take the Ground to dig for Ore;
This Cross gives the <f type="i">Miners<f type="r"> three
days liberty to make and set on
<f type="i">Stows<f type="r">: As many of these Crosses as
the <f type="i">Miner<f type="r"> makes, so many Meers of
Ground he may have in that Vein,
if he set on Stows within three days
after the making his Cross or Cros&shy;
ses; but if he make but one Cross,
and a stander by makes the second,
and a stranger makes the third, eve&shy;
ry one is served with the next Meer,
according as they have first or last,
sooner or later made their Cross or
Crosses upon the Ground.</xpln></wordentry>
</page>

<page sig="f2r">
<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="dish(n)"><f type="i">Dish<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is a Trough made of Wood,
about 28 Inches long, 4 Inches deep
and 6 Inches wide; by which all <f type="i">Mi&shy;
ners<f type="r"> measure their Ore: If any be
taken selling their Ore, not first mea&shy;
suring it by the Bar-masters Dish,
and paying the Kings Duties, the
<f type="i">Miners<f type="r"> incur that forfeiture which
the 17<f type="i">th<f type="r">. Article has imposed upon
them.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="drift(n)"><f type="i">Drift<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is a passage thus <editoraddition>[[image]]</editoraddition> cut
out under the Earth betwixt Shaft
and Shaft, or Turn and Turn, or a
passage or way wrought under the
Earth to the end of a Meer of ground
or part of a Meer.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="farmer(n)"><f type="i">Farmer<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is the Lord of the Field;
or one that farms the Lot and Cope
of the King.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="fodder of lead(n)"><f type="i">Fodder of Lead<f type="r"> </form><xpln>at the Mines con&shy;
tains Twenty two hundred and a
half weight, though in <f type="i">London<f type="r"> but
Twenty hundred weight.</xpln></wordentry>
</page>

<page sig="f2v">
<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="ore, forstid(n)"><f type="i">Forestid Ore<f type="r"></form> <xpln> is Ore that is gotten
out of Earth and Dirt that hath been
washt and Ore taken from it before.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="forefield(n)"><f type="i">Forfield<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is the furthest place that a
man has wrought in his ground; or
the end of a Meer above-ground.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="groove(n)"><f type="i">Groove<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is the Shaft or Pit sunk in&shy;
to the Earth; they are sometimes
sunk in the Vein and sometimes out.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="hack(n)"><f type="i">Hack<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is a Tool that <f type="i">Miners<f type="r"> use
like a Mattock.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="hade(v)"><f type="i">Hade<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is where any Shaft or Turn
goes descending like the side of a
House, or like the descent of a steep
Hill, then it is said to hade.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="load(n)"><f type="i">Load<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is nine Dishes of Ore;
each Dish being about half a hun&shy;
dred weight.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="lob(n)"><f type="i">Lobs<f type="r"> </form><xpln>are steps that ascend or des&shy;
cend within the Mines, as stairs up
to and down from a Chamber.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="lot(n)"><f type="i">Lot<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is the 13<sup>th</sup> Dish, Measure or
Part of the <f type="i">Miners<f type="r"> Ore, which the
Bar-master takes up for the King or
the Farmer.</xpln></wordentry>
</page>

<page sig="f3r">
<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="meer(n)"><f type="i">Meer<f type="r"> </form><xpln>contains Twenty nine yards
in length in any Vein.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="meer-stake(n)"><f type="i">Meer-stake<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is a pin of wood drove
into the superficies of the Earth, to
shew the extent or end of a Meer of
ground.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="works, old(n)"><f type="i">Old works<f type="r"> </form><xpln>are such that are either
fallen in or stand unwrought.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="pipe(n)"><f type="i">Pipe<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is where the Ore runs for&shy;
wards endways in a hole, and doth
not sink downwards or in a Vein.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="pee(n)"><f type="i">Pee<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is where two Veins meet and
cross one another thus &#9769; the place
where they meet is called the <f type="i">Pee<f type="r">.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="possession(n)"><f type="i">Possession<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is the right to a Meer of
ground, which <f type="i">Miners<f type="r"> enjoy, by
having Stows upon that ground;
and it is taken generally for the
Stows themselves; For it is the
Stows that give Possession.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="pawn(n)"><f type="i">Pawn<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is a Pledge of Money put
into the Bar-masters hand at the time
when the Plaintiff causes the Bar&shy;
master to arrest the Mine.</xpln></wordentry>
</page>

<page sig="f3v">
<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="pick(n)"><f type="i">Pick<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is a Tool the Miners use to
cut down the Cliffs and Rocks of
stone to make passages in the Earth.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="rither(n)"><f type="i">Rither<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is a Stone or thin Cliff
that lies in the Vein; The Ore
sometimes runs on both sides it;
Sometimes this Rither is so thick, it
parts the Vein and makes one Vein
two.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="quarter-cord(n)"><f type="i">Quarter-Cord<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is seven yards and a
quarter, which the <f type="i">Miner<f type="r"> hath cross
ways of his Vein on either side; for
liberty to lay his Earth, Stones and
Rubbish on, and to wash and dress
up his Ore.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="rake(n)"><f type="i">Rake<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is the same with a Vein.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="shaft(n)"><f type="i">Shaft<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is the same with a Groove
or Pit; They are sunk some ten some
twenty Fathoms deep into the Earth,
more or less.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="stows(n)"><f type="i">Stows<f type="r"> </form><xpln lexeme="soul-tree(n)" lexeme="stow-blade(n)" lexeme="hang-bench(n)" lexeme="spindle(n)">are seven pieces of Wood
(set upon the superficies of the Earth)
fastened with pins of wood together;
Two are called <f type="i">Soul-trees<f type="r">; two
</page>

<page sig="f4r">
<f type="i">Stow-blades<f type="r">, two <f type="i">Hang-benches<f type="r"> and

ner<f type="r"> or any person that owns them,

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="stool(n)"><f type="i">Stool<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is where the <f type="i">Miners<f type="r"> leave

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="spindle(n)"><f type="i">Spindle<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is a piece of wood fastned
into either <f type="i">Stow-blade<f type="r">.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="smytham(n)"><f type="i">Smytham<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is Lead Ore stamp'd and
pounded down, like Powder or
Sand, to cleanse the Stones and
Earth from the Ore.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="sough(n)"><f type="i">Sough<f type="r"> </form><xpln lexeme="addit(n)">is also called an <f type="i">Addit<f type="r">; It is
a passage like a Vault cut out under
the Earth, to drain the water from

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="smelting-house(n)"><f type="i">Smelting-house<f type="r"></form> <xpln lexeme="fodder(n)"> is a House where

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="sump(n)"><f type="i">Sumps<f type="r"> </form><xpln>are holes sunk in <f type="i">Drifts<f type="r"> to

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="men, twenty-four(n)"><f type="i">Twenty four men<f type="r">, </form><xpln>they are Twenty

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="taker, next(n)"><f type="i">Next Taker<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is he that makes the

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="turn(n)"><f type="i">Turn<f type="r"> </form><xpln>is a Pit sunk in some part of

<wordentry type="h"><form lexeme="wough(n)"><f type="i">Woughs<f type="r"> </form><xpln>are the Walls or sides
