THE 
NEW TESTAMENT 
OF IESVS CHRIST, TRANS&SHY:
LATED FAITHFULLY INTO ENGLISH, 
out of the authentical Latin, 
according to the best cor&shy;
rected copies of the same, diligently conferred with 
the Greeke and other editions in diuers languages: with 
ARGVMENTS of bookes and chapters, ANNOTA&shy;
TIONS, and other necessarie helpes, for the better vnder&shy;
standing of the text, and specially for the discouerie of the 
CORRVPTIONS of diuers late translations, and for 
cleering the CONTROVERSIES in religion, of these daies: 

IN THE ENGLISH COLLEGE OF RHEMES.

Psal. 118.

<term lang="la">Da mihi intellectum, &amp; scrutabor legem tuam, &amp; custodiam
illam in toto meo.</term>

That is,

Giue me vnderstanding, and I wil searche thy law, and
wil keepe it with my whole hart.

S. Aug. tract. 2. in Epist. Ioan.

<term lang="la">Omnia qua leguntur in Scripturis sanctis, ad instructionem &amp; salutem nostrum intent&egrave; oportes
audire: maxime tamen memoria commendanda sunt, qua aduersus H&aelig;reticos valent plu&shy;
rim&ugrave;m: quorum insidia, infirmiores quosque &amp; negligentiores circumuenire non cessant.</term>

That is,

Al things that are readde in holy Scriptures, we must heare with great attention, to our
instruction and saluation: but those things specially must be commended to me&shy;
morie, which make most against Heretikes: whose deceites cease not to cir&shy;
cumuent and beguile al the weaker sort and the more negligent persons.

PRINTED AT RHEMES,
by Iohn Forny.

1582.

<term lang="la">CVM PRIVILEGIO.</term>
