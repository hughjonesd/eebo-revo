<heading>An Explanation 
OF 
Some Terms of the ART of
PAINTING.</heading>

<wordgroup1 type="a">

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="air(n)">Air.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">IS properly taken for the Look of a Fi&shy;
gure, and is used in this Manner, The 
Air of the Heads of Young Women, or 
Grave Men, &amp;c.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="antique(a)">Antique.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">This word Comprehends all the Works of 
Painting, Sculpture, and Archite&shy;
cture that have been made in the Time of 
the Antient Greeks and Romans, from 
Alexander the Great, to the Emperour 
Phocas; under whom the Goths Ravaged 
Italy.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="aptitude(n)">Aptitude.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">It comes from the Italian word <expression lang="it">Attitu&shy;
dine</expression>, and means the posture and action that 
any Figure is represented in.</xpln></wordentry>


</page>

<page sig="c4r">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="cartoon(n)">Cartoon.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">It is taken for a Design made of many 
Sheets of Paper pasted together; in which 
the whole Story to be painted in Fresco, is 
all drawn exactly, as it must be upon the Wall 
in Colours: Great Painters never paint&shy;
ing in Fresco, but they make Cartoons first.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="colouring(n)">Colouring.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">'Tis one of the parts of Painting, by 
which the Objects to be painted receive their 
Complexion, together with their True 
Lights and Shadows.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="chiaroscuro(n)">Chiaro-Scuro.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">It is taken in two Senses: first, Painting 
in Chiaro-Scuro, is meant, when there are 
only two Colours employed. Secondly, It 
is taken for the disposing of the Lights and 
Shadows Skilfully; as when we say, A 
Painter understands well the Chiaro-
Scuro.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="contour(n)">Contour.</form>
<xpln lang="en">The Contours of a Body, are the Lines


</page>

<page sig="c4v">
that environ it, and make the Superficies 
of it.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="design(n)">Design.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Has two Significations: First, As a part 
of Painting, it signifies the just Measures,
Proportions, and Outward Forms 
that a Body, imitated from Nature, ought 
to <emend err="havt">have</emend>. Secondly, It signifies the whole 
Composition of a piece of Painting; 
as when we say, There is great Design 
in such a Piece.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="distemper(n)">Distemper.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">A sort of Painting that implys the Co&shy;
lours mingled with Gumm. And the dif&shy;
ference between that and Miniature, is, 
that the one only uses the Point of the Pen&shy;
cil, the other gives the Pencil its whole Li&shy;
berty.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="drapery(n)">Drapery.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is a General Word for all sorts of Cloa&shy;
thing, with which Figures are Adorn&shy;
ed: So we say, Such a Painter disposes 
well the Foldings of his Drapery.</xpln></wordentry>


</page>

<page sig="d1r">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="figure(n)">Figure.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Though this word be very General, and may 
be taken for any painted Object; yet it is 
in Painting, generally taken for Humane 
Figures.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="fresco(n)">Fresco.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">A sort of Painting, where the Colours 
are applyed upon fresh Mortar, that they 
may Incorporate with the Lime and Sand.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="festoon(n)">Festoon.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is an Ornament of Flowers, employed in 
Borders and Decorations.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="grotesque(n)">Grotesk.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is properly the Painting that is found un&shy;
der Ground in the Ruines of Rome; but it 
signifies more commonly a sort of Painting that 
expresses odd Figures of Animals, Birds,
Flowers, Leaves, or such like, mingled to&shy;
gether in one Ornament or Border.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="gruppo(n)">Gruppo.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is a Knot of Figures together, either in 
the middle or sides of a piece of Painting.


</page>

<page sig="d1v">
So Carache would not allow above three
Gruppos, nor above twelve Figures for 
any Piece.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="history(n)">History.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">History-Painting is an Assembling of 
many Figures in one Piece, to Represent any 
Action of Life, whether True or Fabulous, 
accompanied with all its Ornaments of Land&shy;
skip and Perspective.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="manner(n)">Manner.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">We call Manner the Habit of a Painter, 
not only of his Hand, but of his Mind; that is, 
his way of expressing himself in the three prin&shy;
cipal Parts of Painting, Design, Colou&shy;
ring, and Invention; it answers to Stile 
in Authors; for a Painter is known by his
Manner, as an Author by his Stile, or a
Man's Hand by his Writing.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="model(n)">Model.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is any Object that a Painter works by, either 
after Nature, or otherwise; but most common&shy;


</page>

<page sig="d2r">
ly it signifies that which Sculptors, Pain&shy;
ters, and Architects make to Govern them&shy;
selves by in their Design.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="nudity(n)">Nudity.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Signifies properly any Naked Figure of Man 
or Woman; but most commonly of Woman; as 
when we say, 'Tis a Nudity, we mean the 
Figure of a Naked Woman.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="print(n)">Print.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is the Impression of a Graven or Wooden 
Plate upon Paper or Silk, Representing some 
Piece that it has been Graved after.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="relievo(n)">Relievo.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is properly any Embossed Sculpture that 
rises from a flat Superficies. It is said like&shy;
wise of Painting, that it has a great Relie&shy;
vo, when it is strong, and that the Figures 
appear round, and as it were, out of the Piece.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="mezzo-relievo(n)">Mezzo-Relievo.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is where the Figures rise, but not above 
half of them is seen, the rest being supposed in 
the Marble or Wood.</xpln></wordentry>


</page>

<page sig="d2v">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="basso-relievo(n)">Basso-Relievo,</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is, when the Figures are little more than
Designed, and do rise but very little above 
the Plain: Such are the Figures of the An&shy;
tients about their Cups and other Vessels.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="shortening(n)">Shortning.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is, when a Figure seems of greater quantity 
than really it is; as, if it seems to be three 
foot long, when it is but one: Some call it 
Fore-Shortning.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="stucco-work(n)">Stucco-Work.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is Figures of all sorts, made in a kind of 
Plaister, and employed to Adorn a Room, ei&shy;
ther under the Cornishes, or round the Cei&shy;
ling, or in Compartiments, or Divisi&shy;
ons.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="schizzo(n)">Schizzo.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is the first Design or Attempt of a Painter to 
Express his Thoughts upon any Subject. The Schiz&shy;
zos are ordinarily reduced into Cartoons in Fresco
Painting, or Copyed and Enlarged in Oyl-Painting.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="tinto(n)">Tinto.</form> 
<xpln lang="en">Is, when a thing is done only with one Colour, and 
that generally Black.</xpln></wordentry>
