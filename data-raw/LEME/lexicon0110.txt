<wordgroup1 type="t">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="vowel(n)">A Vowel,</form> <xpln lang="en">is a letter whiche maketh a full and perfect
sounde of himselfe: and there are fiue in number, namely, A,
E, I, O, V: wherevnto is added the Greeke vowell, Y.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="consonant(n)">A Consonant,</form> <xpln lang="en">is a letter which needes must be sounded
with a vowell: as B with E. And all the letters, except the

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="syllable(n)">A Syllable</form> <xpln lang="en">is the pronouncing of one letter or mo, with

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="diphthong(n)">A Diphthonge,</form> <xpln lang="en">is the sound of ij vowels in one Syllable,

Whereunto is added, <term lang="la">ei</term>: as <term lang="la">Aeneus, c&#339;na, audio, euge, hei.</term> </xpln></wordentry>

<section type="Parts of Speech">
<heading>
AN INTRODVCTION 
OF THE EYGHT PARTES OF 
Latine Speache.
</heading>

In Speache be these eight partes fo&#173;
llowinge:

Noune, declined. Aduerb, vndeclined.
Pronoune, declined. Coniunction vndeclined.
Verbe, declined. Preposition, vndeclined.
Participle, declined. Interiection vndeclined.

<wordgroup1 type="a" lang="la" object="Noun"><alpha>Of the Noune.</alpha>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="noun(n)">A Noune</form> <xpln lang="en">is the name of a thinge, that may be seene, 
felte, hearde, or vnderstande: As the name of my 
hande in Latine is <term lang="la">Manus</term>: the name of an house
is <term lang="la">Domus</term>: the name of goodnes is <term lang="la">Bonitas</term>.

Of Nounes, some be Substantiues, and some be Adiectiues.
</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="noun substantive(n)">A Noune Substantiue</form> <xpln lang="en">
is that standeth by him selfe, &#38; requireth 
not another word to be ioyned with him: as <term lang="la">Homo</term>, a man. And it 
is declined with one article: as <term lang="la">Hic magister</term>, a maister. Or else 
with two at the moste: as <term lang="la">Hic &#38; H&#230;c Parens</term>, a father or mother.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="noun adjective(n)">A noune Adiectiue</form> <xpln lang="en">
is that can not stand by himselfe, but requi&#173;
reth to be ioyned with an other word: as <term lang="la">Bonus</term>, Good. <term lang="la">Pulcher</term>, 
Faire. And it is declined either with three Terminations: as <term lang="la">Bo&#173;
nus, Bona, Bonum</term>: or else with three Articles: as <term lang="la">Hic, H&#230;c, &#38; 
Hoc f&#339;lix</term>, Happy. <term lang="la">Hic &#38; h&#230;c leuis</term>, &#38; <term lang="la">hoc leue</term>, Light.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="noun substantive(n)">A Noune Substantiue</form> <xpln lang="en">
either is propre to the thinge that it be&#173;
tokeneth: as <term lang="la">Eduardus</term>, is my proper name, or else is common to 
mo: as <term lang="la">Homo</term>, is a common name to all men.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="case(n)">Cases of Nounes.</form> <xpln lang="en">
NOunes be declined with sixe cases, Singularly and Plurally: 
the Nominatiue, the Genitiue, the Datiue, the Accusatiue, the 
Vocatiue, and the Ablatiue.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="nominative case(n)">The Nominatiue case</form> <xpln lang="en">commeth before the verbe, and aunswe&#173;

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="genitive case(n)">The Genitiue case</form> <xpln lang="en">is knowen by this token Of, and aunswereth

The learning of the maister.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="dative case(n)">The Datiue case</form> <xpln lang="en">is knowen by this token To, and aunswereth

I giue a booke to the maister.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="accusative case(n)">The Accusatiue case</form> <xpln lang="en">foloweth the verbe, &#38; aunswereth to this 
question, whome, or what: as <term lang="la">Amo magistrum</term>, I loue the maister.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="vacative case(n)">The Vocatiue case</form> <xpln lang="en">is knowen by callinge or speakinge To: as 
<term lang="la">O Magister</term>, O maister.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="ablative case(n)">The Ablatiue case</form> <xpln lang="en">is commonly ioyned with Prepositions ser&#173;
uing to the ablatiue case: as <term lang="la">De magistro</term>, Of the maister. <term lang="la">Coram

Also In, with, through, for, from, by, &#38; then, after the compa&#173;
ratiue degree, be signes of ye ablatiue case.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="verb(n)">A Verbe</form> <xpln lang="en" lexeme="doing(n)" lexeme="suffering(n)">is a parte of speache declined with Mode

Of Verbes Personalles there be fiue kindes: Actiue, Passiue, 
Neuter, Deponent, and Commune.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="verb active(n)">A Verbe Actiue</form> <xpln lang="en">endeth in o, and betokeneth to doe: as <term lang="la">Amo</term>, I

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="verb passive(n)">A Verb Passiue</form> <xpln lang="en">endeth in <term lang="la">or</term>, &#38; betokeneth to suffer: as <term lang="la">Amor</term>,

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="verb neuter(n)">A Verbe Neuter</form> <xpln lang="en">

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="verb deponent(n)">A Verbe Deponent</form> <xpln lang="en">

tiue, the Potentiall, the Subiunctiue, and the Infinitiue.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="indicative mood(n)">The Indicatiue mode</form> <xpln lang="en">

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="optative mood(n)">The Optatiue</form> <xpln lang="en">

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="potential mood(n)">The Potentiall mode</form> <xpln lang="en">

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="infinitive(n)">The Infinitiue</form> <xpln lang="en">
