<section type="Title-page">
THE 
Royal Charter 
OF 
Confirmation 
Granted by KING 
Charles II. 
To the CITY of 
LONDON.

Wherein are Recited Verbatim, All the 
CHARTERS to the said CITY, granted by 
His Majesties Royal Predecessors, KINGS and 
QUEENS of ENGLAND.

Taken out of the RECORDS, 
And exactly Translated into English 
By S. G. Gent.

TOGETHER 
With an Index or Alphabetical Table, and 
a Table explaining all the Obsolete and Difficult 
Words in the said CHARTER.

LONDON, Printed for Samuel Lee and Benjamin Alsop, at 
the Feathers in Lombard-street, near the Post-Office, and at 
the Angel in the Poultrey over against the Stocks-market.
</section>

<section type="Glossary">
<heading>
A 
TABLE 
OF 
Obsolete and Difficult words
contained in the Charter.
</heading>

<wordgroup1 type="a" object="A">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en"lexeme="amerciament(n)">AMerciament,</form> <xpln lang="en">a pecuniary punishment in 
Court of an Offender against the King, 
or any other Lord.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="avoirdupois(n)">Avoir-du-pois,</form> <xpln lang="en" lexeme="troy weight(n)">a French word
signifying in 
our Common Law two things: 1. A kind of 
Weight different from that called Troy&shy;
weight, which contains but twelve ounces to 
the pound, whereas this contains sixteen. 
2. It signifies such Merchandizes as are 
weighed by this Weight.</xpln></wordentry>
</wordgroup1>

<wordgroup1 type="a" object="B">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="bridge toll(n)">Brid-toll (or Bridgetoll)</form> <xpln lang="en">a Duty or Tax for
passing over a Bridge.</xpln>
<lemenote>
"Brid-toll" and "Bridgetoll" not found in OED.
</lemenote>
</wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="blank-sterling(n)">Blank-sterling,</form> <xpln lang="en">from the French word <term lang="fr">Blanch</term>, 
i. e. white, a Coin that was coined in the 
parts of France by King Henry the fifth, 
when they were subject to England, the 
value whereof was 8 d. These were for&shy;
bidden to be currant within this Realm 
2 H. 6. The reason why they were called
Blanks, was to distinguish this Coin by its
colour from a certain piece of Gold which 
was then coined at 22 s.</xpln>
<lemenote>
"Blank-sterling" not found in OED.
</lemenote>
</wordentry>
</wordgroup1>

<wordgroup1 type="a" object="C">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="childwite(n)">Childwite,</form> <xpln lang="en">an
old Saxon word signifying a 
power to take a Fine of your Bond-woman 
begotten with child without your consent.</xpln></wordentry>
</page>

<page sig="*1v">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="corrody(n)">Corody,</form> <xpln lang="en">A sum of money, or allowance of 
meat and drink due to the King out of an 
Abby or Religious House whereof he is
Founder, towards the maintenance of such 
one of his servants as he shall think fit to 
make a Pentioner there.</xpln></wordentry>
</wordgroup1>

<wordgroup1 type="a" object="D">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="danegeld(n)">Dane-guilt,</form> <xpln lang="en">a Tribute laid upon our Ancestors
of 12 d. for every Hide of Land through&shy;
out the Realm, by the Danes, for clearing
the Seas (as they pretended) of Pirates.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="denizen(n)">Denizen,</form> <xpln lang="en">i. e. an Alien born, enabled by the 
King's Letters Patents to purchase Lands, 
hold Offices, &amp;c. and his Heirs to in&shy;
herit. But this difference lies, That a per&shy;
son naturalized by Act of Parliament, may 
inherit Lands by descent, which a Denizen 
cannot, though his Heirs may inherit from 
him.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="deodand(n)">Deodand,</form> <xpln lang="en">In Latine, signifies a thing given
or forfeited to God for the pacification of 
his wrath, for any accident whereby a Chri&shy;
stian comes to his death without the default 
of any reasonable creature. As if a Horse 
kill his Keeper, or a Cart run over a man, 
the Creatures and Instruments are forfeited
to the King to be distributed to the poor.</xpln></wordentry>
</wordgroup1>

<wordgroup1 type="a" object="F">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="folkmote(n)">Flackmote, or Folkmote,</form> <xpln lang="en">a Saxon word, sig&shy;
nifying a Court holden in London, wherein 
all the people of the City did complain of 
Irregularities and Mis-governments with&shy;
in the City.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="frank-pledge(n)">Frank-pledge,</form> <xpln lang="en">a Pledge or Surety for Free&shy;
men of fourteen years of age (except Clerks
and Knights) their finding Sureties to&shy;
wards the King and His Subjects, or else
</page>

<page sig="*2r">
they were to be sent to prison.
</xpln></wordentry>
</wordgroup1>

<wordgroup1 type="a" object="G">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="guild(n)">Guild,</form> <xpln lang="en">a Fraternity or Company combined to&shy;
gether by Orders and Laws made among 
themselves by the Prince's Licence.</xpln></wordentry>
</wordgroup1>

<wordgroup1 type="a" object="H">
<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="husting(n)">Husting,</form> <xpln lang="en">a French word, signifying the prin&shy;

</wordgroup1>

<wordgroup1 type="a" object="I/J">

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en" lexeme="infang thief(n)">Infangtheft,</form> <xpln lang="en">a Saxon word, signifying a Liber&shy;

</wordgroup1>

<wordgroup1 type="a" object="K">

</wordgroup1>

<wordgroup1 type="a" object="L">
