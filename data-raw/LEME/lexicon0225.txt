<section type="preface">
<heading>
<f type="i">A Table, shewing the signification of sundry forraine
<f type="r">words, vsed in these discourses.
</heading>

<f type="bk">G<f type="r">Entle Reader, for as much as in these Military discourses, I
haue vsed some words and termes, somwhat straunge vnto
such as haue not frequented forraine warres, nor haue anie
great insight in forraine languages, I haue therefore thought 
good both to declare the signification of such words, and al&shy;
so to shew my reasons for vsing the same.

First you are to vnderstand that most of our termes now
vsed in warres are deriued from straungers;as the French, the
Italian, the Spaniard and the Dutch; wherin euery one almost haue their seuerall
pronuntiation; therefore I hold it good, to vse such war termes and words,as
we do borrow from straungers (as most languages doe borrow some, more or
lesse, one of another)neare after the same nature, orthographie &amp; accent, as those
nations do, from whom they are deriued : and not to pronounce and vse them o&shy;
uer corruptly,as we commonly doe. As for example. The word <term lang="it"><f type="i">Caporall<f type="r"></term>, which is
a meere Italian, and also vsed by the French,we corruptly do both write and pro&shy;
nounce <term lang="en"><f type="i">Corporall<f type="r"></term> : for <term lang="it"><f type="i">Caporall<f type="r"></term> doth signifie the head and chiefe of a squadra,
or small company of souldiers :and is an officer ensigning and gouerning (in his
degree) the sayd company : and is in Spanish more aptly called <term lang="sp"><f type="i">Cabo de esquadra<f type="r"></term> 
which is the head of a small squadra of 20 or 25 souldiers. And againe , wee
vse both to pronounce and write, <term lang="fr"><f type="i">Core de guard;<f type="r"></term> which by the French is written
<term lang="fr"><f type="i">Corps de guard,<f type="r"></term> and by the Italian and Spaniard, <term lang="it"><f type="i">Corpo de guardia;<f type="r"></term> which signi&shy;
fieth the body of a watch : the which French or Italian word I haue rather vsed in
my writing, then our corrupt English terme. Moreouer wee both pronounce
and write the word <term lang="en"><f type="i">Canuasada,<f type="r"></term> the which(in truth)ought to be written and pro&shy;
nounced <term lang="sp">Camisada</term>, being a Spanish tearme; and doth signifie the inuesting a
shirt ouer the soldiers apparell or armour; the which is vsed in the night time
when any suddaine exploit or peece of seruice is to be put in practise vpon the e&shy;
nemy, vnexpected or vnseene: to the end, that in the darkenesse of the night, the
attempters may thereby the better one know and discerne another. These few
words and termes, I suppose sufficient for my reasons. And for the signification
of all other straunge and forraine words by me vsed, I will here following parti&shy;
cularly set downe, in order of alphabet,as I shall call them to memory. 
</section>
