<section type="Title-page">
&para; The Breuiary of 
Britayne.

As this most noble and renow&shy;
med Iland, was of auncient 
time deuided into three King&shy;
domes, England, Scotland and 
Wales.

Contaynyng a learned discourse 
of the variable state, &amp; altera&shy;
tion therof, vnder diuers, as 
wel natural: as forren prin&shy;
ces, &amp; Conquerours. 

Together with the Geographicall de&shy;
scription of the same, such as neither 
by elder, nor later writers, the 
like hath been set foorth 
before.

Writen in Latin by Humfrey 
Lhuyd of Denbigh, a Cambre 
Britayne, and lately Englished 
by Thomas Twyne, 
Gentleman.

1573.
</section>
</page>
