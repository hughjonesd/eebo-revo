<section type="preface">
Since nothing could so well express the meaning and intention of the Author, as the very <f type="i">Latin<f type="r"> 
and <f type="i">Greek<f type="r"> Words used in these Tracts, we have continued them expresly, and though in many 
Places I have given their meaning by <f type="i">Synonyma's<f type="r">, yet for the benefit of meer <f type="i">English<f type="r"> Readers, we 
have here composed a Table Alphabetically of all the hard <f type="i">Greek<f type="r"> and <f type="i">Latin<f type="r"> Words, used in 
the whole Volume; as also of all Terms of Art, and many other Words derived from the 
<f type="i">Latin<f type="r"> and <f type="i">Greek<f type="r">, tho' usual among Scholars, yet not frequently known to the vulgar, and 
therefore we have fully explained them, and rendred them intelligible to the meanest Capacity 
in the following Table. </section>
