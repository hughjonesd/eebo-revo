<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en">Amorons,</form> <xpln lang="en">are certaine fleshie excrecenses, at the beginning like Mulberries,

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en">Glans,</form> <xpln lang="en">is the Acorne or head of a mans Yard.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="la">Aloperia gallica,</form> <xpln lang="en">is meant the falling of the haire in this Disease.</xpln></wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en">Spanish-Buttons, Pustills, and Spanish Fleas,</form> <xpln lang="en">are drie Scabbes appearing

</lemenote>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="en">Talpa,</form> <xpln lang="en">is a virulent Disease, and fouleth the vpper table of the Cranium or

<lemenote>
"Talpa" in this sense antedates the earliest OED citation (1684).
</lemenote>
</wordentry>

<wordentry type="h"><form lang="la">Periostion,</form> <xpln lang="en">is a skinne that couereth all the bones of the body of man, and
